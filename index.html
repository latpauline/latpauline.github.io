The Power of Ordinary Filipinos

Where Unity Became Power

Pauline Lat | BSIT 2-A | February 2026

When I learned about the 1986 EDSA People Power Revolution, the moment that touched me the most was seeing how ordinary Filipinos stood bravely in front of military tanks along Epifanio de los Santos Avenue. From February 22 to 25, 1986, millions of citizens gathered peacefully to protest the dictatorship of President Ferdinand Marcos. What makes this event powerful is that the people did not fight with violence. Instead, they brought rosaries, food, flowers, and prayers. Some even offered flowers to soldiers and spoke to them calmly despite the danger. It proved that collective faith and bravery can overcome fear.

Historicizing EDSA, Source: Panay News

It amazes me that the revolution lasted only four days, yet it changed the direction of our country. The dictatorship that lasted for twenty years came to an end, and democracy was restored. But beyond the political result, what inspires me most is the unity of the people. Students, workers, parents, priests, nuns, and even young individuals stood together with one goal. They showed that courage is not about fighting back with force. Sometimes courage means standing firm without hurting others.

“EDSA reminds us that democracy survives when citizens participate.”

As a citizen of our country and part of the younger generation, I sometimes feel that my voice is small and does not matter. However, EDSA reminds me that change begins with ordinary people who care. Democracy is not something that automatically continues forever. It survives when citizens are active, responsible, and informed. In a time when misinformation spreads quickly on social media, remembering EDSA encourages me to verify information, think critically, and vote wisely in the future, especially this upcoming 2028 National Elections.

For me, the 40th anniversary of EDSA is not just about remembering the past. It is about asking myself what kind of citizen I want to be. The revolution teaches me that freedom requires participation and that unity remains one of the strongest powers a nation can have. Lastly, I firmly believe that;

“We Filipino's must think like a man of action, and act like a man of thoughts.”

About This Blog

This blog shares a personal reflection on the EDSA People Power Revolution and its lasting relevance to today’s generation as we commemorate its 40th anniversary. It explores how the values of unity, courage, faith, and active citizenship continue to guide young Filipinos in protecting democracy and shaping the nation’s future.

40th Anniversary

40th Anniversary 2026 marks four decades since the peaceful revolution that restored democracy in the Philippines. In 1986, millions of Filipinos united along EDSA in a historic display of courage and faith, ending years of authoritarian rule. As we commemorate its 40th anniversary, we are reminded that democracy is not a one-time achievement but a continuous responsibility. The spirit of People Power lives on whenever citizens stand for truth, justice, and national unity.
